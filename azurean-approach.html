about azurean

India Focussed Investment Manager

Azurean Investment Managers is a young company, founded by investment professionals with decades of experience of investing in Indian listed equities.

Azurean Investment team aims to generate wealth for its investors in sync with India's progress by investing in high quality companies across the market cap segments that offer a good value. As we believe India has strong growth potential owing to its demographics, that presents opportunities to invest across consumption, services and investment sectors. We look for generating alpha by effective sector allocation among large well established companies. Our approach to mid and small companies is driven by bottom up company specific strengths. Growth at reasonable price, and Free cash generation with reasonable return on capital employed are our key guiding principles.

Vison - Our goal is to build a world class investment management firm recognized for its excellence in capital allocation efficiency.
Mission - To attract passionate investment professionals committed to fostering wealth creation.

Investment Philosophy

Bottom Up

  • Our approach in selecting stocks in the mid and small cap space will be on a bottom-up.
  • Aim is to generate wealth by backing good quality businesses over the long period.
  • Consistent free cashflow, reasonable Return on capital employed (RoCE), and Management quality will be the key factors in our decision making
  • The team has decades of experience in understanding businesses across diverse sectors focusing on nuances that affect their profitability and long-term value creation potential.
  • The big money is not in buying and selling…but in the waiting Jesse Livermore

Top Downn

  • Our approach towards large cap companies will be more top-down
  • Look for segments of the economy that are exhibiting strong growth momentum to narrow down on the companies that are best positioned to capitalize.
  • Demand-supply balances, capacity additions & utilization, pricing power to decide sectoral allocations.
  • The approach would be to combine macroeconomic analysis with sector-specific insights.
  • Risks would be mitigated by ensuring diversification at the portfolio levels.

Our approach to portfolio construction

Investment Philosophy

  • Growth at reasonable price (GARP) will be the key tenet of our Investment Philosophy
  • Long term orientation - Buy good quality business even at slightly higher prices and sit on them for long periods
  • Free cash generation – Buy businesses that generate ample free cash flow and where the Return on equity is significantly above the cost of capital to ensure long term value creation
  • Governance – Invest in companies that have a proven track record of strong corporate governance

Portfolio Construction

  • Our portfolio will primarily focus on large, well-established companies with a proven track record
  • We will seek out companies that possess competitive advantages such as strong brands, wide distribution networks, superior technology, and cost efficiencies with entry barriers.
  • Key Investment factors:
    • - Look for companies that are positioned favourably in business cycle
    • - Take tactical exposures based on specific events or special situations
    • - Take exposures with a value bias or contrarian approach selectively

Our approach to portfolio construction

Businesses that we like

  • Scalable Businesses with large Market Opportunity : Look for businesses that can grow significantly and tap into large markets.
  • Pricing Power and Cost Management : Companies that can pass on cost increases to customers demonstrate strong pricing power.
  • Financial Strength : Prioritize companies with robust free cash flow generation and high return on capital employed.
  • Effective Management and Governance : Seek well managed companies with high standards of governance
  • Innovation and Competitive Advantage : Consider innovative firms with sustainable competitive advantages.
  • Sunrise Industries : Explore sectors with a long growth runway.

Businesses that we look to avoid

  • Complex Corporate Structures : Avoid companies with intricate holding structures.
  • Capital-Intensive Businesses with Low ROCE : Be wary of capital-intensive businesses that reinvest most of their profits to sustain operations but have low returns on capital employed.
  • Capital Allocation Concerns : Avoid companies that allocate excess cash poorly, especially those engaging in unrelated diversification instead of dividends or buybacks.
  • Governance and ESG : Avoid companies that score poorly on governance and neglect environmental, social, and governance (ESG) factors while conducting business.
  • Sunset Industries : Avoid industries where the terminal value is at risk due to declining relevance.

Risk management

Diversification
Individual stocks carry risk, but a well-diversified portfolio can reduce that risk.
Time Horizon
As the time horizon increases, the probability of negative returns of a portfolio decreases
Leverage
There is no leverage employed at the portfolio level, further reducing risk.
Equities
Equities are risky due to their volatility, but not investing in them is riskier as one may loose purchasing power due to inflation!
